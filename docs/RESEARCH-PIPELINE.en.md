# Chinese Thought Atlas Research Data Pipeline

Version: `method-v1`  
Effective: 2026-07-14

## Data layers

- `data/research/` is the research layer and may contain incomplete, duplicate, disputed, or excluded records.
- `data/*.csv` is the publication layer generated by `npm run research:publish`.

The frontend and API consume publication data only. Candidate people, bibliographic leads, workflow states, and placeholder prose must never become statement nodes.

## Unit of research

A statement node is an attributable philosophical proposition supported by evidence and independently open to acceptance or rejection. A field, school label, biography, bibliography, isolated aphorism, or unsourced internet quotation is not a statement.

The registry may include philosophers and non-philosophers whose work materially shaped philosophical problems. Fame, office, or one popular quotation is insufficient.

## Workflow

```text
person registry → source discovery → work and attribution registry
→ source excerpt and locator → candidate atomization → canonical formulation
→ within-person deduplication/splitting → Chinese review → English semantic review
→ relation evidence review → publication gate → frontend CSV/JSON/API
```

Automated search, language models, general encyclopedias, and similarity scores may generate leads only. They cannot directly create published claims or historical-influence assertions.

| `release_manifests.csv` / `release_items.csv` | reproducible publication manifests |

## Deduplication

- Merge claims by the same person and phase when their truth conditions are equivalent.
- Original text, modern Chinese paraphrase, and English translation are three texts for one claim, not three claims.
- Split propositions that can be independently accepted or rejected.
- Usually separate a definition from a normative conclusion inferred from it.
- Preserve major changes across intellectual phases.
- Never deduplicate similar claims across people; connect them as similarity, extension, contrast, or rejection.

Every merge, split, and exclusion decision remains in the research layer.

## Source tiers

1. Primary text or reliable digital text;
2. Critical edition or authoritative translation;
3. Peer-reviewed specialist scholarship or university-press monograph;
4. Scholarly references such as SEP, IEP, and Oxford;
5. Institutional profiles and research databases;
6. General encyclopedias, search results, and aggregators for discovery only.

Legendary figures, lost texts, and traditional attributions require explicit historicity, authorship, and evidence limitations.

## Publication gate

A claim may be `published` only when it is a position; its person is registered; it has a reviewed locator; it is an independently assessable proposition; within-person deduplication is complete; Chinese and English semantics are reviewed; reviewer and date metadata are complete; attribution disputes are disclosed; `research_status=canonical`; and `npm run research:validate` passes.

Relations require two published endpoints, an explicit subtype, bilingual notes, and acceptable evidence. Conceptual similarity must not be promoted to historical influence automatically.

## Current baseline

As of 2026-07-14, all 155 people are in both the research registry and publication layer, with 815 person-source or corpus-search leads, 756 person-by-dimension coverage records, 167 bilingual source-located claims, and 14 claim relations. Every person has at least one position; the original six core figures retain multiple positions. Legendary personae, later encounter records, disciple notes, and serious secondary summaries are explicitly distinguished rather than presented as contemporary verbatim speech.

“Source lead identified” does not mean “independently peer reviewed.” The 167 claims pass the current structural, bilingual, locator, deduplication, and attribution-disclosure gates; edition-level, translation, and whole-system coverage review remains ongoing. Reports must expose registry size, people with published claims, excerpt count, and pending gates separately.
